    • Ants belong to the family Formicidae of superfamily Vespoidea of the insect order Hymenoptera. The Formicidae contains 296 extant genera under 16 subfamilies worldwide and constitutes one of the most important eusocial vespoid aculeates with a wingless worker caste, forming perennial colonies. There are an estimated 15,000 living ant species, of which only around 9,000 to 10,000 have been described.
